Privacy Policy

Waymark · Last updated: April 2026

Waymark is a travel-tracking app for iOS. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it is used, and your rights as a user.

What data Waymark collects

1. Country-level location

When automatic location capture is enabled, Waymark uses your device's location to determine which country you are in — once in the morning and once in the evening. Only the inferred country code (e.g. "US", "DE") is stored. Raw GPS coordinates are never saved to disk or transmitted anywhere.

2. Travel records you create manually

If you add a log manually, Waymark stores the date, country code, and any note you choose to write. This data lives entirely on your device.

3. Goals

Goal settings (country, target days, date range) are stored locally on your device.

4. Diagnostic data

Waymark records internal events (e.g. capture timestamps, error codes) in a local log for debugging purposes. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to any server.

How your data is stored

On your device. All data is stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework. No data is sent to external servers operated by the developer.

iCloud Sync (optional). If you choose to enable iCloud Sync in Settings, your travel records and goals are synced across your own devices via your personal iCloud account. This data is stored in a private CloudKit container associated with your Apple ID — the developer cannot access it.

Data sharing

Waymark does not:

  • Share your data with third parties
  • Sell your data
  • Use your data for advertising or profiling
  • Send your data to any developer-operated server

Deleting your data

You can delete all app data at any time from Settings → Your Data → Reset App. This clears all local records. If iCloud Sync was enabled, you can also remove the app's iCloud data from iOS Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage.

Children's privacy

Waymark is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from children.
